Artist Bio: Kendra Yoshizawa

Kendra Yoshizawa, a Japanese-Canadian artist currently based in Vancouver, Canada, is celebrated for her distinctive artistic style, best described as "vaguely impressionist." Drawing upon a rich blend of cultural influences and a comprehensive education in both fine arts and architecture, Kendra's work embodies a fusion of mediums and techniques that captivates and enchants.

  From a young age, Kendra displayed a remarkable affinity for art, embarking on her creative journey at ages 8 to 14. During this formative period, she explored an array of artistic mediums, including graphite, ink, pastel, acrylic, watercolor, and oil painting, setting the stage for her lifelong passion.

  Graduating high school with the prestigious "District Dogwood Scholarship in Fine Arts," Kendra's dedication to her craft led her to pursue a deeper understanding of art and its integration with the built environment. She continued her studies at the University of British Columbia, studying architecture and further refining her artistic expression.

  During her high school years, Kendra had the invaluable opportunity to work closely with the late muralist Alan Wylie, who served as her mentor. Under his guidance, she honed her skills and enriched her artistic vision. Kendra made significant contributions to Alan Wylie's mural project for the Langley Centennial Museum, leaving an indelible mark on the community through her artistry.

  In addition to traditional mediums, Kendra explored sculptural art using clay, wood, and metal, showcasing her versatility and ability to breathe life into three-dimensional forms. She also embraced digital tools, skillfully employing them to create intricate graphics, 3D models, drafting, and rendering.

  Further showcasing her commitment to embracing innovation, Kendra pursued studies in software engineering, enhancing her proficiency in merging creativity with cutting-edge technology. This multidimensional approach to art underscores her adaptability and forward-thinking mindset.

  Kendra Yoshizawa's artwork is a testament to her cultural heritage and her deep appreciation for the beauty inherent in the world around us. Her "vaguely impressionist" style invites viewers to immerse themselves in a vivid tapestry of color, texture, and emotion, leaving a lasting impression that resonates on a profound level. As she continues to evolve as an artist, Kendra remains dedicated to pushing the boundaries of her craft, experimenting with new techniques, and creating art that inspires and uplifts all who have the privilege of experiencing it.
